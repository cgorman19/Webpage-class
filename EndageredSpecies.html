Javan Rhino - Rhinoceros Sondaicus

WHERE ARE THEY FOUND?

Ujung Kulon National Park, Java, Indonesia

They used inhabit a much larger area, spreading across countries, like India, Bhutan, Bangladesh, China, Myanmar, Thailand, Loas, Cambodia, Vietnam, Malaysia and Indonesia.

Basic Facts

Status: Critically Endangered
Population: 58-68
Height: 4.6-5.8 feet
Weight: 1,984-5071 pounds
Length: 10-10.5 feet
Habitat: Tropical Forest
Scientific Name: Rhinoceros Sondaicus
Life Span: 30-40 years

Where Did They Go?

The main reason for the decrease in Javan Rhino population is poaching. The horn of the rhino is used for medicines in Asian contries, it is also a sign of wealth and status. Another cause was deforestation, taking away their habit takes away their source of life.

What's Being Done to Stop It?

WWF continues to track and do research on the rhinos. WWF has also started restoring the habitat for the rhinos. An invasive spices moved in and eats all the food, WWF is using their resources to try and removed the invasive animal to help the Javan.
